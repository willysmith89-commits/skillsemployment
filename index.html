What's inside the course

Four units. Two credentials. Job-ready skills.

Your course is made up of the three nationally recognised units of the CHCSS00114 skill set, plus your in-person First Aid & CPR unit.

CHCCCS015

Provide individualised support

The core support-worker skill — helping people with daily living safely and with dignity. It's the unit employers look for first.

HLTINF006

Apply basic principles and practices of infection prevention and control

Keep yourself and your clients safe — essential practice in every aged care and disability setting.

HLTWHS002

Follow safe work practices for direct client care

Work safely around direct client care — the foundation of every shift you'll ever work.

HLTAID011

Provide First Aid — including CPR

Hands-on, in person with Australia Wide First Aid — the certificate every care employer asks for.

All three CHCSS00114 units provide credit towards the CHC33021 Certificate III in Individual Support. Verify the skill set any time on the national register at training.gov.au.

Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions

When are classes held?
Live online classes run Monday to Wednesday, 9:00am–12:00pm each week, with new intakes starting every Monday (Sydney time). Your practical First Aid & CPR session (HLTAID011) is completed in person after you finish the online training. You'll need a computer with your camera on during classes (a compliance requirement) and at least 80% attendance to complete the skill set.
How much does the course cost?
Pay in Full is $400 — one payment today, your best-value option. Or choose the Easy Start Plan: $100 today, then five easy weekly payments of $70 ($450 total). The $50 difference is simply the convenience of flexible payments. Both options include exactly the same course and support.
Why can't I do First Aid & CPR before I've paid in full?
Your course fees must be paid in full before you attend the First Aid and CPR component — it's a firm requirement with no exceptions. Pay $400 in full and you're covered from day one. On the Easy Start Plan, your place is confirmed as soon as your fifth $70 payment clears. We'll keep you posted so nothing catches you by surprise.
Is this course nationally recognised?
Yes. The CHCSS00114 Entry into Care Roles Skill Set is Nationally Recognised Training delivered by Contour College (Contour Systems Pty Ltd), RTO 90504 — verify any time at training.gov.au. Your HLTAID011 Provide First Aid (including CPR) is delivered in person by Australia Wide First Aid, RTO 31961, at locations across Australia.
Do I need any experience or qualifications to start?
No. This course is designed for beginners and career-changers. If you can commit to three mornings a week online, you can do this course.
What happens after I finish?
You finish with two nationally recognised credentials — your CHCSS00114 Statement of Attainment and your HLTAID011 First Aid certificate — so you can start applying for aged care jobs right away. You'll also have a one-on-one session with our careers consultant to talk through your options, and if you choose to continue, the units you've completed are credited toward the CHC33021 Certificate III in Individual Support — you start the full qualification ahead of the pack.
How do online payments work?
You pay through Stripe's secure checkout — card, Apple Pay or Google Pay. Pay in Full is $400 once. On the Easy Start Plan you pay $100 today; your card is securely saved and the 5 remaining payments of $70 are charged automatically, one week apart. Your enrolment is confirmed by email the moment your payment clears — even overnight.
